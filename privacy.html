QBO Enterprise Dashboard

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 10, 2026

Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how QBO Enterprise Dashboard collects, uses, stores, and protects your information when you use our Service.

1. Introduction

QBO Enterprise Dashboard ("Service," "we," "us," or "our") is a multi-company consolidated reporting dashboard available at consolidatedreport.app, with backend services hosted at overflowing-ambition-production-4b7e.up.railway.app. The Service connects to QuickBooks Online ("QBO") via Intuit's OAuth 2.0 API to retrieve and consolidate financial reports across multiple QBO companies.

By using the Service, you consent to the data practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use the Service.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of information:

Account Information

  • Email address — used for login and account identification
  • Password — stored as a one-way SHA-256 hash; we never store your plaintext password

QuickBooks Online Data

  • OAuth 2.0 tokens (access token and refresh token) — used to authenticate with the Intuit API on your behalf
  • Company identifiers (Realm IDs) for your connected QBO companies
  • Financial report data — Profit & Loss statements, Balance Sheets, and Cash Flow statements retrieved from your connected QBO companies

Technical Information

  • Session tokens — used to maintain your authenticated session
  • Server logs — standard request logs (IP address, timestamp, request path) generated automatically by our hosting infrastructure

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect solely to provide and operate the Service:

  • Authentication — to verify your identity and manage access to your account
  • QBO API Access — to connect to your QuickBooks Online companies, retrieve financial report data, and refresh OAuth tokens as needed
  • Report Consolidation — to aggregate and display financial data from multiple QBO companies in a unified dashboard
  • Performance — to cache report data locally, reducing redundant API calls and improving load times
  • Security — to detect and prevent unauthorized access, fraud, or abuse

We do not use your information for advertising, marketing, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to delivering the Service.

4. QuickBooks Online Data

We take the handling of your QuickBooks data seriously and adhere to Intuit's developer requirements:

  • We access only the financial report data necessary to provide consolidated reporting (Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow)
  • We do not modify, create, or delete any records in your QuickBooks Online accounts — our access is read-only
  • We do not sell, rent, lease, or share your QuickBooks financial data with any third party
  • We do not use your QuickBooks data for any purpose other than displaying it to you within the Service
  • You may revoke the Service's access to your QBO companies at any time through your Intuit account settings or by disconnecting the company within the Service

Upon disconnection or revocation, we will delete the associated OAuth tokens and cease accessing data from that company.

5. Data Storage & Retention

Your data is stored as follows:

  • OAuth tokens — stored in a SQLite database on our backend server and retained only as long as your QBO company connection is active
  • Cached report data — financial report data is cached in SQLite to improve performance; cached data is refreshed periodically and deleted when you disconnect a company or delete your account
  • Account credentials — your email and hashed password are stored in SQLite and retained until you request account deletion

Upon account deletion or termination, all associated data — including OAuth tokens, cached reports, and account information — will be permanently deleted within 30 days.

6. Data Security

We implement commercially reasonable security measures to protect your data:

  • All communication between your browser and our servers is encrypted via HTTPS/TLS
  • Passwords are stored as one-way SHA-256 hashes and cannot be reversed
  • OAuth tokens are stored server-side and never exposed to the client browser
  • Access to the backend server and database is restricted to authorized personnel only

While we strive to protect your information, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, but we are committed to promptly addressing any security incidents that may arise.

7. Cookies & Tracking

We do not use cookies for advertising, analytics, or tracking purposes. The Service uses only session tokens to maintain your authenticated login state. No third-party tracking scripts, pixels, or analytics tools are embedded in the Service.

8. Third-Party Services

The Service integrates with the following third-party services:

  • Intuit / QuickBooks Online API — We connect to QBO through Intuit's official OAuth 2.0 API to retrieve your financial data. Your use of QuickBooks Online is governed by Intuit's Privacy Statement and Intuit's Terms of Service.
  • Railway (Hosting) — Our backend is hosted on Railway's infrastructure. Railway processes data on our behalf in accordance with their security practices.
  • Netlify (Hosting) — Our frontend is hosted on Netlify. Netlify may collect standard web server logs.

We do not share your personal data or QuickBooks financial data with any other third parties, except as required by law.

9. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information or financial data to anyone. We may disclose your information only in the following circumstances:

  • Legal compliance — if required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request
  • Safety and rights — to protect the rights, property, or safety of our users, the public, or ourselves
  • Service providers — to infrastructure hosting providers (Railway, Netlify) who process data on our behalf and are bound by confidentiality obligations
